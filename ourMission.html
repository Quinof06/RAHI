ROCHESTER AFFORDABLE HOUSING, INC.

Mission Statement

Accepted June 17, 2019

Last Amended July 22, 2019

The Town of Rochester has a Housing Production Plan (June 2010) addressing the issues of affordable housing as a whole. Rochester Affordable Housing, Inc. (RAHI) was formed to specifically address the need for affordable housing for its senior citizens, those over the age of Sixty-two (62), RAHI does not address the overall needs of affordable housing for Rochester. It does prioritize the needs of its senior citizens but recognizes that others may be served if inventory of affordable housing can be made available.

The RAHI will follow the same qualifying criteria as outlined in the Rochester Production Plan, but as a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, it will have more flexibility in creating and maintaining the needed affordable housing inventory.

The RAHI will work with the Rochester Housing Opportunity Partnership (HOP) and any other organization, public or private, whose goal is to meet the over all need for affordable housing with senior citizen needs remaining the first priority for the RAHI.

RAHI Priorities:

  1. Rochester residents or those having ties to Rochester sixty-two (62) years of age or older
  2. Rochester residents or those having ties to Rochester who are disabled veterans of the United States Military services.
  3. Rochester residents or those having ties to Rochester who are honorably discharged veterans of the United States Military service.
Our Vision of affordable housing for seniors and veterans of Rochester takes on many different forms. All of these have a general idea of including a small but comfortable, one bedroom, handicap unit with accessibility to amenities in the area within walking distance or by transit. These units would be built on a condominium-type style where maintenance is included.
